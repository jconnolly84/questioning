TECHNIQUE • COLD CALLING

Cold Calling

Selecting pupils to answer (with warmth and support) so everyone stays engaged. Done well, it normalises thinking out loud and widens participation.

Video

Watch the demonstration, then use the notes and resources to plan how you’ll run it tomorrow.

How to run it (quick routine)

  • Set the rule: everyone thinks first (no hands up unless invited).
  • Ask one clear question; pause long enough for real thinking.
  • Collect responses from across the room (not just keen hands).
  • Probe: “How do you know?” “What makes you say that?” “Can anyone improve it?”

Why it works (when it’s warm)

Cold calling widens participation and keeps everyone switched on — but only if pupils feel supported. The goal is “everyone thinks” not “catch someone out”. Pair it with thinking time and follow-up questions.

  • Stops hands-up domination.
  • Normalises speaking in full sentences.
  • Lets you check understanding across the room.

No Opt Out (the safety net)

If a pupil is stuck, you don’t move on and leave the gap. You support them to get there:

  • Ask a second pupil for help (“Who can support?”)
  • Return to the first pupil: “Now you say it.”
  • Praise the effort: the win is joining in.

Best follow-ups

  • “What makes you say that?”
  • “Where’s the evidence?”
  • “Can you improve that answer?”
  • “Do you agree or disagree — and why?”
