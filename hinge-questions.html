TECHNIQUE • HINGE QUESTIONS

Hinge Questions

A single, carefully chosen question that tells you whether the class is ready to move on — or needs reteaching — before you commit to the next step.

How to run it (quick routine)

  • Set the rule: everyone thinks first (no hands up unless invited).
  • Ask one clear question; pause long enough for real thinking.
  • Collect responses from across the room (not just keen hands).
  • Probe: “How do you know?” “What makes you say that?” “Can anyone improve it?”

Why hinge questions are powerful

A hinge question is a decision point. It tells you if pupils are ready for the next step. If the hinge shows a gap, you reteach now — not after the test. Done well, it prevents lessons drifting on top of misunderstandings.

  • It’s fast (30–90 seconds).
  • It targets a common misconception.
  • It leads to an immediate action: move on, reteach, or split the class.

Design checklist

  • One concept only (no multi-skill questions).
  • Wrong answers represent real misconceptions.
  • Pupils can answer in under 30 seconds.
  • You already know what you’ll do for each outcome.

Easy formats

  • Multiple choice with misconception options.
  • True/False + “fix it” sentence.
  • Pick the best example/non-example.
