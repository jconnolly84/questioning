TECHNIQUE • POSE–PAUSE–POUNCE

Pose. Pause. Pounce.

Ask the question, give real thinking time, then select pupils to answer. It raises participation and quality because the pause stops ‘fastest wins’.

Video

Watch the demonstration, then use the notes and resources to plan how you’ll run it tomorrow.

How to run it (quick routine)

  • Set the rule: everyone thinks first (no hands up unless invited).
  • Ask one clear question; pause long enough for real thinking.
  • Collect responses from across the room (not just keen hands).
  • Probe: “How do you know?” “What makes you say that?” “Can anyone improve it?”

Why it works

This routine builds in the two things most classrooms miss: thinking time and accountability. The pause improves answer quality; the pounce widens participation because everyone has had time to prepare.

  • Higher-quality answers (less blurting, more reasoning).
  • More pupils stay engaged because anyone could be selected.
  • Cleaner behaviour: the room feels purposeful during the pause.

Question stems that work brilliantly

  • “What is the most important point and why?”
  • “Which option is best? Convince me.”
  • “What’s the misconception here?”
  • “If that’s true, what must also be true?”

Make it sharper

During the pause, tell pupils what thinking looks like: write 5 words, underline the key term, or choose A/B/C with a reason. Then when you pounce, you get evidence-based answers — not vibes.
