TECHNIQUE • THINK–PAIR–SHARE

Think–Pair–Share

A simple structure that gets every pupil thinking, talking, and refining their idea before anyone answers publicly. Great for EAL/SEND because it gives rehearsal time.

Video

Watch the demonstration, then use the notes and resources to plan how you’ll run it tomorrow.

How to run it (quick routine)

  • Set the rule: everyone thinks first (no hands up unless invited).
  • Ask one clear question; pause long enough for real thinking.
  • Collect responses from across the room (not just keen hands).
  • Probe: “How do you know?” “What makes you say that?” “Can anyone improve it?”

Why it works

Think–Pair–Share forces silent thinking first, then gives pupils a low-pressure rehearsal with a partner. By the time you go public, answers are clearer, longer, and more accurate — especially for EAL/SEND pupils who benefit from structured talk.

  • Stops “fastest hand wins” and reduces guessing.
  • Improves vocabulary and sentence quality through rehearsal.
  • Lets you circulate and listen for misconceptions before feedback.

Try it tomorrow

  • Think (10–20s): write a bullet answer (no talking).
  • Pair (30–60s): agree one best answer + one example.
  • Share: cold call 2–3 pairs: “What did you agree and why?”
  • Upgrade: ask for improvements: “Make it more precise.”

Common pitfalls (and fixes)

  • It turns into chatting: give a timer + specific output (“one sentence + one example”).
  • Only confident pupils share: cold call pairs, not volunteers.
  • Answers stay vague: use prompts: “Because…”, “For example…”, “This shows…”
